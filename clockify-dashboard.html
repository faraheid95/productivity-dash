Attribution Model — No Silent Assumptions
How each ticket is credited to a developer

Step 1 — QA-flow handoff (high confidence): For each closed ticket, assignment history is fetched from Azure DevOps. If the ticket was ever assigned to Aesha Hassen or Ijaz Ahmed (testers) or Sameh Amnoun (code reviewer), the person who was assigned directly before that transition is credited as the delivering developer. green QA% = ≥ 70% of tickets via this method.

Step 2 — Most-recent assignee (inferred, lower confidence): If no QA handoff is found in the ticket's history, the most recent non-QA, non-reviewer assignee is credited. These are flagged with grey QA% in the table and in quality signals.

Exclusions: Aesha Hassen & Ijaz Ahmed (QA testers) and Sameh Amnoun (code reviewer) are excluded from attribution in all cases — they are the gate that triggers attribution, not the recipients of it. Tickets with no identifiable developer are unattributed (9 tickets, not shown in table).

Role classification: Delivery-heavy = ticket count×2 + PRs authored > 1.2× review score and ≥8 tickets or PRs. Review-heavy = ≥8 review/approve actions and review score > 1.5× delivery score. Balanced = neither threshold. Role is based on ticket attribution + PR activity — it reflects contribution pattern, not seniority.

Quality signal thresholds: Reopen rate ≥15% = High · 8–14% = Medium. Cycle time > 1.6× team average flagged. 0% QA confidence flagged as low-confidence. ADO-only contributors flagged for time-tracking follow-up. All thresholds are visible — no hidden penalties.
